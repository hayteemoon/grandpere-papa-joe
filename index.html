Tribute to Grandpere, Pastor Joseph Aniel Angerville

About Pastor Joseph Aniel "Papa Joe" Angerville

Pastor Joseph Aniel "Papa Joe" Angerville was born in the island country of Haiti: February 2, 1928. He was a God-loving-and-fearing man, instilling the Christian faith in the heart of every one of his children. Pastor Angerville lived a long 97 years as a "gentle soul" on Earth and each birthday was certainly celebrated as a blessing to his relatives, coworkers, and friends. Toward the latter years of his life, the reverend was surrounded by immediate family. Loving the LORD, he was entrusted with an abundant family lineage of sons, daughters, grandsons, granddaughters. Pastor Joseph Aniel Angerville passed away in an Orlando, Florida hospice care facility called AdventHealth on May 26, 2025. Fortunately enough, he was entombed beside his wife, Marie C. Angerville.

Achievements

  • Blessed by God to birth and raise an "Angerville Nation" worldwide in Haiti, France, Florida, New York, Korea, and beyond including 9 children (8 boys and 1 girl) full of accomplished professionals like musicians, poets, authors, accountants, armed forces veterans, etc. Papa Joe was selfless, generous, and passionate about education, fostering a drive in his children to attend and finish their schooling.
  • Pastored and ministered in the Haitian Christian church till 1968 and partnered with US Baptist Missions as a "servant and Man of God", "disciple of Christ", "brother", and "friend of the church" where he was greatly beloved, "singing with passion...even while he was in his hospital bed". His most cherished hymn was "It is Well With My Soul". One of his most favored verses was Matthew 4:4: "But Jesus told him, 'No! The Scriptures say, ‘People do not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God.’”
  • Enjoyed employment to provide for his family as he interpreted for American missionaries and migrated to the United States, beginning social work in New York City until retirement servicing youth, disabled children, and displaced peoples in group homes.
  • Had a vision for all his children to live in the United States while the country was at its peak. Resultingly, he gave all of his children English names and succeeded in bringing his household to the U.S. by the end of the 70s era.
  • Avid, expert player of dominoes, Haitian cards "Bezique", soccer, volleyball, etc.
  • Known as "The Father of the Block".
